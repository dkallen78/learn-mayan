Objectives

  • At the end of this unit the students will be able to distinguish the tonal pronunciations and will have learned the Mayan alphabet
  • The students will be albe to use the personal pronouns and dependent pronouns
  • The students will be able to use the dependent pronouns as possesive adjectives
  • The students will begin to conjugate the verbs in the present current and in the present habitual
  • The students will be able to formulate questions with the following adverbs: bix, tu'ux and ba'ax

The Mayan Alphabet

The Mayan alphabet consists of 24 letters and the sign (') which denotes a glottal consonant.

Consonants

b ch ch' h k k' l m n p p' s t t' ts ts' w x y

The d, f, g, r, and rr are borrowed from Spanish to write the words that have been introduced to Mayan.

Vowels

a e i o u

These vowels are pronounced according to the location of the accents which determine their tone.

áa àa a'a áa' a'
ée èe e'e ée' e'
íi ìi i'i íi' i'
óo òo o'o óo' o'
úu ùu u'u úu' u'

Each consonant can be combined with vowels to obtain names or monosyllabic or bisyllabic words. The placement of the accent determines the tone of vowel.
