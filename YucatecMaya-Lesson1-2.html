Conjugations in Mayan

Present Indicative

The present indicative is divided into current and habitual

Present Current

The present current indicates that the action of the verb is executed in the same moment it is expressed. To conjugate the verbs in the present current tense, first the personal pronoun is said ( tèen, tèech, leti', to'on, te'ex, letio'ob), then add the particle táan, the dependant pronoun (in, a, u, k, a, u), and finally, the verb (míis: to sweep).

Conjugation Model

Personal Pronoun Particle Dependant Pronoun Verb
1st Person Singular tene'táaninmíis
2nd Person Singular teche'táanamíis
3rd Person Singular leti'e'táanumíis
1st Person Plural to'one'táankmíis
2nd Person Plural te'exe'táanamíise'ex
3rd Person Plural letio'obe'táanumíiso'ob

All verbs that begin with a vowel take the following prefixes (a'alik: to say).

Personal Pronoun Particle Dependant Pronoun Verb
1st Person Singular tene'táaninwa'alik
2nd Person Singular teche'táanawa'alik
3rd Person Singular leti'e'táanuya'alik
1st Person Plural to'one'táanka'alik
2nd Person Plural te'exe'táanawa'alike'ex
3rd Person Plural letio'obe'táanuya'aliko'ob

The particle táan can contract and convert to tin. For example, eliminate part of the personal pronoun and the particle táan, and what's left is the first letter of the personal pronoun (t) and the dependant pronoun (in).

Contraction Verb
1st Person Singular tinmíis
2nd Person Singular tamíis
3rd Person Singular tumíis
1st Person Plural tmíis
2nd Person Plural tamíise'ex
3rd Person Plural tumíiso'ob

The forms with the particle táan indicate an action constantly progressing. It's an action necessarily of more than a momentary duration going in the present time.

Present Habitual

The present habitual does not indicate when the action of the verb is executed, rather, it only expresses a habitual action.

The present habitual differentiates itself by the particle k that precedes the dependant pronouns in place of the t of tin that is used in the present current.

Contraction Verb
1st Person Singular kinmíis
2nd Person Singular kamíis
3rd Person Singular kumíis
1st Person Plural kmíis
2nd Person Plural kamíise'ex
3rd Person Plural kumíiso'ob

The forms that begin with the particle k indicate a habitual or general action and is not necessarily happening in that moment.

The dependent pronouns convert into possessive adjectives when they are put before nouns. All of the nouns that begin with a vowel take the prefix w or y (otoch: house).

Possessive Noun
1st Person Singular inwotoch
2nd Person Singular awotoch
3rd Person Singular uyotoch
1st Person Plural kotoch
2nd Person Plural awotoche'ex
3rd Person Plural uyotocho'ob

More examples
in pòol my head
a na' your mother
u yotoch his/her house
k suku'un our brother
a kìike'ex your sister
u yíits'ino'ob their little brother

The nouns are pluralized with the suffix o'ob.

Singular Plural
hen kàax kàaxo'ob
house nah naho'ob
dog pèek' pèek'o'ob
cat mìis mìiso'ob
rabbit t'u'ul t'u'ulo'ob
turtle áak áako'ob
bird ch'íich' ch'íich'o'ob
iguana hùuh hùuho'ob
table mayek mayeko'ob

When the number of animals, persons, or things is indicated, it isn't necessary to use the suffix o'ob. For example:

tin manik ka'atúul kàax
I am buying two hens
tin manik kantúul mìis
I am buying four cats
